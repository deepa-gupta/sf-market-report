March 2026 Market Commentary

📊

Market Overview

San Francisco real estate continues to show resilience heading into spring. Inventory remains constrained across all price points, keeping competitive bidding conditions intact — particularly for well-priced single family homes in core neighborhoods.

📊

What's Driving the Market

The rate environment has stabilized enough to bring sidelined buyers back into the market. We're seeing stronger attendance at open houses compared to Q4 2025, and multiple-offer situations are common for move-in ready homes priced under $2M. Sellers who price correctly are routinely closing above asking within two weeks.

🏠

Single Family Homes

Demand for single family homes in Noe Valley, Bernal Heights, and the Sunset remains elevated. The $1.5M–$2.5M range is the most competitive segment, with buyers frequently waiving contingencies to win. Homes with outdoor space and ADU potential continue to command premiums.

🏢

Condos

The condo market is more nuanced. High-rise units in SoMa and the Financial District are moving, but with less urgency — buyers have more leverage here than they did 18 months ago. Mid-rise buildings in Noe Valley, Pac Heights, and the Castro are performing better, often matching the energy of the single family market.

🔭

Looking Ahead

Spring typically brings an increase in both listings and buyer activity. Watch for pent-up inventory to hit the market in April and May. Buyers who acted in Q1 may have had less competition; sellers considering a spring listing should price carefully — the market rewards fair pricing and punishes overreach.
