San Francisco Real Estate Market Commentary — March 2026

📊

Where the Market Stands

If you've been watching and waiting, spring 2026 is giving you a clearer picture. Across 9,114 closed sales through March 19, average prices have risen to $2.21M over the past 90 days — up 17% from a year ago. Homes are also selling faster, down from 68 days on market to 66. The market is moving, and the direction is up.

📌

If You're Buying a House

Competition for single-family homes is real. The average buyer is paying 17.8% above asking price — compared to just 3.9% a year ago. That means if a home is listed at $1.5M, expect to offer closer to $1.77M to be competitive. Homes in Noe Valley, Pacific Heights, and Bernal Heights are seeing the most activity, with prices averaging $2.79M, $3.81M, and $1.59M respectively. If you're serious, get fully pre-approved before you look — not after.

🏢

If You're Buying a Condo

You have more breathing room. Condos are averaging 2.7% over asking (versus 17.8% for houses), and homes are sitting on the market about 70 days before going under contract. South Beach has the highest volume with 58 sales averaging $1.38M, and Nob Hill is active at $1.83M. This is one of the better windows for condo buyers in recent years — you can take time to inspect, negotiate, and be selective.

📌

If You're Selling

The data is in your favor, especially for houses. Buyers are waiving contingencies and stretching their budgets to win. The key is pricing accurately at or just under market value to attract multiple offers — homes that are overpriced sit, lose momentum, and ultimately close for less. The homes selling fastest and highest are the ones that feel move-in ready and are priced to invite competition.

📌

The Seasonal Moment

December and January were quieter — overbids averaged around 3%. February jumped back to 9%, and March's early data is tracking at 13%. This is the spring surge. If you're planning to list, now through May is historically when you'll see the strongest buyer interest and the most competitive offers.

Buyer Insight — Choosing Your Type

Single-family homes sell faster and command a premium — but 78% close over asking at an average overbid of +22.4%. Condos offer more negotiating room: 49% sell under asking.

Seller Insight — Pricing Strategy

Single-family homes priced at or slightly below market trigger the most competitive bidding. With 78% selling over asking, strategic under-pricing drives the best outcomes.
